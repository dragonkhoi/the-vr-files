About The VR Files

The VR Files is a curated collection of quality research and information pertaining to industry VR. These files have been aggregated and synthesized over time by people like you looking for validation of immersive technologies. Each file is written in blog style, detailing key takeaways and important findings. Full-text for studies and articles is included, along with APA citations. The site is organized by industry: ALL, HEALTHCARE, TRAINING, EDUCATION, and BRANDING. Each category contains articles that have been tagged as potentially applicable to that industry.

I started The VR Files because I have repeatedly heard about the need for a database of research findings in VR to validate, inspire, and inform applications of immersive technology across a wide variety of fields. The papers I've collected here are from my colleagues at the Stanford Virtual Human Interaction Lab as well as various pieces I've cited in my time doing research on VR.
